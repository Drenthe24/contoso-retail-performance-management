Design Principles
General principles behind the visual style
Purpose of the theme

The Contoso Tech Dark theme was created for an executive-style performance management dashboard. Its goal is not to make the report decorative, but to make key metrics, trends and outliers easy to read at a glance.

The dark background keeps the interface quiet, while the accent colors guide attention toward the numbers and business signals that matter most.

Focus

Important elements use strong contrast and brighter colors. Supporting information stays neutral and does not compete for attention.

Consistency

The same metric, category or business concept should keep the same visual treatment across the whole report.

Hierarchy

Color, typography and spacing are used to clearly separate KPIs, titles, labels, axes and supporting notes.

01
Keep colors consistent across pages
A category, region or business concept should keep the same color wherever it appears in the report.
02
Avoid random palettes
Avoid relying on automatically assigned Power BI colors when the visual represents important business categories.
03
Use heatmaps to show intensity
For matrices and heatmaps, use controlled gradients. The goal is to highlight concentration and relative weight, not to create a rainbow effect.
5
Typography
Font hierarchy and readability
Font roles
UsageFontDescription
Page titlesSyneUsed for main titles and high-impact callouts. It gives the dashboard a distinctive identity.
Body / visual textInter or Segoe UIUsed for descriptions, labels and standard report content. The priority here is readability.
Technical labelsJetBrains MonoUsed for badges, dates, codes, small labels and metadata. It creates a subtle technical feel without distracting from the business content.
⚠
Power BI Service may not always render custom fonts depending on the browser or tenant configuration. For standard visuals, Segoe UI is a safe fallback.
